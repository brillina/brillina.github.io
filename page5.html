Takeaways

  • There is no direct correlation between mask usage and COVID-19 cases, as there are counties with lower weighted mask usage and higher reported numbers of deaths, and vice versa.
  • There also seems to be no trend between the vulnerability of a county and the reported COVID-19 cases. However, some states have more vulnerable counties than others, but the number of reported cases remains persistent.
  • Population may have a stronger effect on cases. For many states, counties with a lower population are more clustered near the origin which indicates fewer reported cases as well.
  • We might draw the conclusion that the number of COVID-19 deaths per county corresponds more to its population and geographical location (the county's distance from a major city) than to the frequency of mask usage and SVI score.
